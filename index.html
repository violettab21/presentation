Scrum Overview

Scrum is a framework that is used by teams to deliver complex projects. The idea is that team is delivering small peaces of work (Increments) in short cycles called Sprints

Increment - valuable shippable product

Increment is a valuable shippable product that is extended each sprint. At the end of each sprint Increment is presented to the stakeholders. It gives a chance to receive a valuable feedback on the early stages.

Scrum Ceremonies

  • Sprint Planning
  • Daily Stand-up
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retro

Sprint Planning

Purpose: define what scrum team will deliver by the end of the sprint

Sprint planning is an event that is performed in the beginning of the sprint. Using this event team plans which tasks are committed to be completed be the end of the sprint

What is needed for successful Sprint Planning?

  • Prepared backlog items
  • Full Scrum Team
  • Take into account team capacity
  • Think about dependencies between items
  • Do not overcommit
  • Focus on Sprint Goal
To have productive planning, team should have prepared and prioritized backlog items. Whole team should participate in discussion.

How scrum was applied on real project?

Sprint Planning

  • Tickets are prepared
  • Past sprint statistics prepared

Project 2

  • 1 hour
  • Tickets have high-level description
  • Team is estimating tickets

Project 1: Performed during first day of the sprint. All scrum teams participated and committed to certain list of tickets. Tickets are estimated and prioritized in advance.

Project 2: Performed during first day of the sprint. All scrum teams participated and committed to certain list of tickets. Tickets are estimated during planning, description of tickets can be changed on planning. Open questions are often raised by team members

Daily Stand-up

Purpose: Catch up with the team, check if team is on track with sprint goal achievements

Short 15 mins meeting where team discuss progress and raises concerns and notifies about blockers

What should be discussed on Daily?

  • Progress of work
  • Inform team regarding blockers
  • Share updates that can be useful for the team

How scrum was applied?

Sprint Review

Purpose: Show Sprint Results to stakeholders and teammates and receive feedback

It's usually demo sessions where teams and customers meet and see results of work, ask questions and provide feedback. It may happen that new backlog items can be created based on feedback. It takes place at the end of the sprint

What can we show on sprint review?

  • Diagrams with completed tasks/resolved bugs
  • Functionality of implemented User Stories
  • Focus on business value during demonstration
  • Invite customers to provide feedback

Sprint Retrospective

Purpose: Discuss how successful sprint went and improve the process of work

Retro is supposed to help teams to understand what can be improved in process of work to achieve better results. It conducted in the end of the sprint.

What to do on retro?

  • Create Retro Board
  • What we need to start doing?
  • What we need to stop doing?
  • What we need to continue doing?
  • Invite team members for voting
  • Create action items
  • Assign and track action items

Sprint Retrospective

Project 1

  • Once per 2 sprints
  • Action items were created based on discussion

Project 2

  • First day of the sprint
  • No clear action items
  • Sprint Retro and Planning on the same day

Project1: productive discussions, action items are created and tracked

Project2: team has 2 ceremonies in one day. Team is not motivated to actively participate.

Scrum Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Flexible
  • Constant feedback is received from the customer
  • Working product is available in short period of time
  • Transparent

Disadvantages

  • Many meetings
  • Knowledge of SCRUM framework required
  • Applicable for small teams

When to use?

  • Dynamic Projects
  • Requirements changes are expected
  • Quick result is required
